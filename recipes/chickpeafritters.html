Chickpea Fritters

Ingredients

1 unit Shallot

1 unit Lemon

2 clove Garlic

1/4 ounce Dill

13.4 ounce Chickpeas

2 tbsp Sour Cream

4 tbsp Yogurt

82g Tempura Mix

1 tbsp Shawarma Spice Blend (2 part tumeric, 2 part cumin, 1 part dried coriander, 1 part garlic powder, 1 part paprika, .5 part ground allspice, .5 part black pepper

Salt

Sugar

Pepper

2 tsp Olive Oil

1 tsp Cooking Oil

Utensils

  • Zester
  • Strainer
  • Medium Bowl
  • Potato Masher
  • Small Bowl
  • Whisk
  • Large Pan
  • Paper Towel

Instructions

  1. Wash and dry produce. Halve, peel and finely chop half the shallot, thinly slice remaining. Zest and halve the lemon. Peel and mince garlic. Pick and finely chop fronds from dill. Drain and rince chickpeas; transfer to medium bowl. Mash with potato masher or fork until almost smooth.
  2. In a small bowl, combine shallot and juice from half the lemon. Season with salt and a pinch of sugar. Set aside to pickle. In a separate small bowl, ocmbine sour cream, yogurt, lemon zest, half the chopped dill, and a pinch of garlic to taste. Add water 1 tsp at a time until mixture reaches a drizzling consistency. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. In a second medium bow, whisk together tempura mix, shawarma spice, salt (~3/4 tsp), and 1/3 cup cold water until smooth. If mixture is too thick, add 1 tbsp of water at a time until reaches pancake batter like consistancy. Stir in chickpeas, chopped shallot, remaining garlic, and remaining chopped dill until throughly combined. Season with salt and pepper. Wash out bowl used for chickpeas
  4. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large, preferably nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Carefully add 1/4 cup spoons of batter (in batches) Cook until golden brown and crisp, 3-4 minutes per side. Transfer fritters to a paper-towel lined plate. Season with a pinch of salt. Turn off heat.
